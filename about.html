About Me

Hi! I’m Shobha.

Growing up with an undiagnosed autoimmune disease, I interacted with hospitals and insurance more often than any 12-year-old should. These experiences opened my eyes to the inefficiencies in patient care and lit a spark in me to reform the “user experience” of healthcare, especially for patients with chronic conditions.

Since then, I’ve explored every angle of the healthcare system that I could – wet lab life sciences research, health policy, digital health startups, patient advocacy groups, AI in healthcare, and public health. Of those, I am especially drawn to digital health startups for their direct, innovative impact on patients and policy/government for the ability to make large-scale, systemic changes – and I especially believe in the synergy between both of these worlds.

I am now the Co-Founder of Flair Health, which is a virtual platform offering nutrition counseling and health coaching services for patients with chronic conditions. I graduated from Stanford, where I studied Computer Science and Ethics and earned recognition as a Fulbright Scholar Semi-Finalist. I currently live in San Francisco and enjoy exploring coffee shops, learning new dance styles (salsa and bachata are my current favorites), and volunteering in my community.

In the past:

  • Studied Computer Science + Ethics and Philosophy + Healthcare Innovation + a bunch of other fun things at Stanford.
  • Product & Ops at early-stage digital health startups (Songbird Therapy, Rupa Health) and LinkedIn
  • Author of “Hacking Healthcare,” a book about utilizing technology to improve the “user experience” of healthcare.
  • Published commentaries about public-private partnerships in healthcare in the New England Journal of Medicine and Nature.
  • Founded an edtech company focused on training the next-generation of spelling bee champions (and hosted a national spelling bee).
